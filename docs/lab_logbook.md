## Week 1: Project Scoping & Research Design (Nov 1-7, 2025)

### Day 1 (Nov 1): Problem Identification
**Objective:** Identify a meaningful problem for portfolio project

**Activities:**
- Brainstormed potential problem areas: food delivery, productivity tools, local services, job search
- Reflected on personal frustrations with productivity tools (Notion, Todoist, Trello)
- Validated problem significance by researching industry abandonment rates
- **Decision:** Focus on productivity tool abandonment in first 14 days

**Insights:**
- Industry baseline: ~82% overall abandonment, 18% Day-14 retention
- Personal experience: Abandoned 5+ tools myself
- Market size: $100B+ productivity tools market

**Next Steps:** Design research methodology

---

### Day 2-3 (Nov 2-3): Research Methodology Design
**Objective:** Create rigorous research plan

**Activities:**
- Designed interview guide (semi-structured, 30-45 min)
- Identified research questions:
  - Why do users abandon?
  - What specific moments trigger abandonment?
  - What emotional factors drive the decision?
- Planned participant recruitment strategy
- Determined sample size: 22 interviews (enough for saturation)

**Key Decisions:**
- ‚úÖ Qualitative over quantitative (need deep understanding, not just metrics)
- ‚úÖ Behavioral segmentation (not demographic)
- ‚úÖ Focus on emotional drivers (not just functional failures)

**Artifacts Created:**
- Interview script (15 questions)
- Participant screening criteria
- Research plan document

---

### Day 4-5 (Nov 4-5): Participant Recruitment
**Objective:** Recruit 22 diverse participants

**Activities:**
- Posted recruitment messages in student groups, LinkedIn, Reddit
- Screened participants (criteria: 18-28 years old, abandoned 2+ productivity tools)
- Scheduled interviews over 4-week period
- Prepared consent forms and recording setup

**Recruitment Breakdown:**
- 40% engineering/CS students (The Overwhelmed Optimizer type)
- 35% general college students (The Serial Abandoner type)
- 25% creative professionals/grad students (The Analog Holdout type)

**Challenges:**
- Initial low response rate ‚Üí Added $10 Amazon gift card incentive
- Scheduling conflicts ‚Üí Offered flexible evening/weekend slots

---

### Day 6-7 (Nov 6-7): Pilot Interviews
**Objective:** Test and refine interview guide

**Activities:**
- Conducted 3 pilot interviews with friends
- Refined questions based on feedback
- Adjusted interview flow (moved emotional questions earlier to build rapport)

**Key Learnings:**
- Starting with "tell me about yourself" was too cold
- Better opener: "Have you ever abandoned a productivity app? Tell me about that."
- Needed more probing questions for emotional states
- 30-45 minutes was appropriate length

**Interview Guide v2 Created** (finalized)

## Week 2-4: Data Collection (Nov 8 - Nov 28, 2025)

### Daily Interview Log

#### Week 2 (Nov 8-14): Interviews 1-6
**Pattern Emerging:** Users blame themselves, not tools

**Notable Quotes:**
- INT_001: "I'm just not disciplined enough to use these apps"
- INT_003: "Everyone else seems to have their life together with Notion"
- INT_005: "I spend 3 hours setting up, then never actually use it"

**Emotional Tone:** Self-criticism, guilt, frustration

---

#### Week 3 (Nov 15-21): Interviews 7-14
**Pattern Emerging:** Setup fatigue is universal

**Notable Observations:**
- 11/14 participants mentioned watching YouTube tutorials (2-5 hours)
- 9/14 spent 2-4 hours on initial setup
- 12/14 described feeling "overwhelmed by options"

**Insight Forming:** Complexity is the enemy, not lack of features

---

#### Week 4 (Nov 22-28): Interviews 15-22
**Pattern Emerging:** Abandonment happens in 2-week window

**Data Points:**
- Average abandonment time: 12.3 days
- 14/22 abandoned before Day 14
- 18/22 abandoned before Day 21

**Counter-Intuitive Finding:** "More features" was cited as negative, not positive

## Week 5: Data Analysis & Synthesis (Nov 29 - Dec 5, 2025)

### Day 1-2 (Nov 29-30): Transcription & Initial Coding
**Objective:** Prepare data for analysis

**Activities:**
- Transcribed all 22 interviews (used Otter.ai for initial pass, manually cleaned)
- First-pass coding: Highlighted key quotes and observations
- Created observation repository (180+ discrete observations)

**Workflow:**
- Read each transcript fully
- Highlighted meaningful statements
- Extracted to separate document with interview ID

**Output:** 187 discrete observations

---

### Day 3-4 (Dec 1-2): Affinity Mapping
**Objective:** Cluster observations into themes

**Activities:**
- Printed all 187 observations on sticky notes (digital version in Miro)
- Initial clustering by similarity
- Refined clusters, merged similar themes
- Named 8 major themes

**Themes Identified:**
1. Feature Overwhelm (82% of interviews)
2. Productivity Guilt (68%)
3. Setup Fatigue (73%)
4. Context Switching (55%)
5. Prioritization Difficulty (64%)
6. Tool Hopping Behavior (88%)
7. Social Comparison Anxiety (41%)
8. Lack of Flexibility (45%)

**Key Insight:** Top 3 themes (overwhelm, guilt, setup) account for abandonment

---

### Day 5 (Dec 3): Persona Development
**Objective:** Create behavioral personas

**Activities:**
- Grouped interview participants by behavior patterns (not demographics)
- Identified 3 distinct behavioral archetypes
- Created detailed persona profiles with goals, frustrations, typical day

**Personas Created:**
1. **The Overwhelmed Optimizer** (40% of sample)
   - Downloads every app, abandons in 2 weeks
   - Spends more time organizing than doing
   
2. **The Serial Abandoner** (35% of sample)
   - Enthusiastic start, guilt-driven abandonment
   - Blames self for failure
   
3. **The Analog Holdout** (25% of sample)
   - Prefers paper, tried digital, reverted
   - Values flexibility over features

**Validation:** Showed personas to 5 participants ‚Üí 100% said "this is exactly me"

---

### Day 6-7 (Dec 4-5): Journey Mapping
**Objective:** Map current state and ideal future state

**Activities:**
- Created current state journey (6 stages: Discovery ‚Üí Abandonment)
- Identified critical moments and pain points
- Designed future state journey (6 stages: Discovery ‚Üí Long-term use)
- Mapped interventions at each stage

**Critical Moments Identified:**
- **First 5 minutes:** Empty workspace ‚Üí guided onboarding
- **Day 3-5:** Reality check ‚Üí Show wins, not failures
- **Day 10-14:** Abandonment window ‚Üí Weekly reflection

## Week 6: Insights Synthesis & PRD Creation (Dec 6-15, 2025)

### Day 1-2 (Dec 6-7): Key Insights Development
**Objective:** Synthesize research into actionable insights

**Activities:**
- Reviewed all data (interviews, affinity map, personas, journey maps)
- Identified counter-intuitive patterns
- Quantified pain point prevalence
- Validated insights against raw data

**7 Key Insights Generated:**
1. Critical 14-day window
2. Feature paradox (less = more)
3. Guilt-driven abandonment
4. Setup fatigue prevents usage
5. First 24 hours predict success
6. Self-attribution bias
7. Context-switching needs

**Validation Method:** Each insight supported by 3+ data points

---

### Day 3 (Dec 8): Problem Statement Refinement
**Objective:** Craft precise, measurable problem statement

**Iterations:**
1. ‚ùå "Productivity tools are hard to use"
2. ‚ùå "Users abandon productivity tools"
3. ‚úÖ "Students and young professionals abandon productivity tools within the first 14 days because initial setup complexity and task visibility creates cognitive overload and guilt, leading to 82% abandonment rate"

**Why This Works:**

### Day 4-5 (Dec 9-10): Solution Design
**Objective:** Design solution addressing root causes

**Activities:**
- Brainstormed 20+ potential solutions
- Evaluated each against research findings
- Selected 5 core principles:
  1. < 2-minute onboarding
  2. 3-task visibility limit
  3. Progressive feature disclosure
  4. Anti-guilt design
  5. First-session success

**Key Design Decisions:**
- ‚úÖ Constraints over freedom (prevents overwhelm)
- ‚úÖ Progressive over complete (reduces cognitive load)
- ‚úÖ Celebration over punishment (prevents guilt)
- ‚ùå Rejected: Gamification (could create guilt if missed)
- ‚ùå Rejected: Social features (comparison anxiety)
